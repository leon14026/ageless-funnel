It's Not Your Fault — A Woman's Body Needs a Different Approach

Teenage hormone changes, pre- and post-pregnancy recovery, and menopause can all change your energy, weight, mood, joints, and confidence. You're not failing — your body needs guidance that understands a woman's real life.

🔬

Hormones Change Everything

Estrogen helps control metabolism and how your body stores fat — especially around the midsection. These are clinical realities, not personal failures.

🦴

Your Joints Deserve Clinical Care

High-impact exercises designed for women in their 20s can cause real damage to a menopausal body. You need biomedically scaled, joint-safe movement — not generic gym routines.

💪

Muscle Loss Threatens Your Independence

Sarcopenia — age-related muscle loss — can quietly reduce strength decade by decade. Without structured, progressive resistance training, you risk losing the mobility and independence you've always had.

🧠

Depression Risk Rises

Low energy, body changes, poor sleep, and constant stress can feed anxiety, depression, and low confidence. Your routine should support calm, resilience, and emotional wellbeing too.

That's exactly why Coach Tulee and Biomedical Engineer Leon designed this program — to work with your body's changes, not against them.

For over 35 years, Coach Tulee — the Most Certified Female Fitness Consultant of Bangladesh and an MA graduate in English Literature from Dhaka University — has approached fitness as both an art form and a human narrative. The Road That Built Coach Tulee

From a young karate champion to a coach, gym owner, educator, survivor, and international medalist, Shamima Akhtar Tulee's story is the foundation of Ageless by Tulee.

1989 Karate champion years

Breaking the first barrier

At a time when martial arts was not an expected path for Bangladeshi women, Shamima Akhtar Tulee became the first woman in Bangladesh to earn a Shotokan karate black belt. That achievement was only the beginning.

Before turning 18, she became a national karate champion, then held the national title five consecutive times from 1989 through 1993. Those years built the discipline, courage, and technical standard that would shape her future as a coach.

2001 From champion to coach

Opening a space for women to train

Tulee founded Combat Gym by Tulee in 2001, turning her martial arts experience into a practical coaching home for women who wanted strength, confidence, and structure.

For more than two decades, the gym became a place where thousands of women could learn movement with discipline and care. Her work expanded beyond training sessions into books, health columns, national television, and public education.

2000s Health crisis and resilience

Learning recovery from the inside

Tulee's authority is not only professional. During pregnancy, a hormonal complication and medical neglect at a critical moment changed her health and forced her through years of illness, surgeries, and recovery.

Rebuilding her own strength safely gave her a personal understanding of fear, pain, patience, and the feeling that your body has stopped cooperating. That lived experience became central to the way she coaches women today.

2022 UNESCO ICM / MARIE

Taking the work to an international stage

In 2022, Tulee represented Bangladesh through UNESCO ICM's Martial Arts Research Initiative for Experts, connected to the International Martial Arts Academic Seminar in South Korea.

Her work focused on youth development through martial arts, extending the same belief that shaped her gym: disciplined movement can build confidence, character, and long-term health far beyond the training floor.

2022 Ozawa Cup, Las Vegas

Returning to the international podium

In Las Vegas, Tulee competed in the veteran kata category at the Ozawa Cup International Karate Tournament and won a bronze medal. It was a historic return after everything her body had endured.

Leon stood beside her in that achievement, making their journey a rare mother-son martial arts milestone. The moment proved something personal: recovery was not only possible, it could lead back to strength.

Now Ageless by Tulee

Turning the journey into a system

Ageless by Tulee grew from this full journey: the champion years, decades of coaching, the health crisis, the recovery, and the return to international competition.

Today, Tulee and Leon use that lived experience to help women move safely, rebuild confidence, and stop feeling alone in a changing body. The program is built for women who need structure, empathy, and proof that strength can return.
